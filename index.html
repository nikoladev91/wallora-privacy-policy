Privacy Policy for Wallora

Last updated: July 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Wallora ("the App") accesses, uses, stores and shares information when you use the App.

1. Developer and Privacy Contact

Developer: Nikola Ławniczak
Country: Poland
Email: wallora.support@gmail.com

2. Information Provided Directly by Users

Wallora does not require users to create an account. The App does not ask users to provide their name, postal address, phone number, email address or payment card information.

If you contact the developer by email, the information contained in your message will be used only to respond to your inquiry and provide support.

3. Advertising and Automatically Collected Information

The free version of Wallora displays advertisements provided by Google AdMob.

Google and its advertising partners may automatically collect or process information such as:

  • advertising identifiers;
  • IP address and approximate location derived from the IP address;
  • device and operating system information;
  • app interactions and advertisement interactions;
  • diagnostic, performance and crash information;
  • information used for fraud prevention and security.

This information may be used to provide personalized or non-personalized advertisements, measure advertising performance, prevent fraud, maintain security and improve advertising services.

Google processes this information according to its own policies. You can learn more at:

Google Privacy Policy

Additional information about how Google uses information from apps that use its services is available at:

How Google uses information from sites or apps that use its services

4. Consent and Advertising Choices

Where required by law, users may be asked for consent before data is used for personalized advertising or related purposes.

Users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom and Switzerland may be shown a consent message provided through Google's consent management tools.

Where available, users may review or change their privacy and advertising choices through the Privacy and cookie settings option provided in the App.

5. Local App Data

Wallora may store certain preferences locally on the user's device, including:

  • favorite wallpapers;
  • theme or display preferences;
  • other basic application settings.

This locally stored information is not transmitted to the developer. It can normally be removed by clearing the App's data or uninstalling the App.

6. Wallpaper Downloads and Device Storage

When a user downloads a wallpaper, the image is saved locally on the user's device.

The developer does not receive a list of the wallpapers downloaded by the user.

Depending on the Android version and device configuration, the App may request access necessary to save images to the device. Wallora does not use this access to collect or transmit the user's personal photos.

7. Third-Party Services

Wallora uses services provided by third parties, including:

  • Google AdMob;
  • Google Play services.

These providers may process information according to their own privacy policies and legal obligations.

8. Data Sharing

The developer does not sell users' personal information.

Information may be processed or shared with Google and its advertising partners when necessary to provide advertising, measure performance, prevent fraud, maintain security and operate the services described in this Privacy Policy.

Information may also be disclosed when required by applicable law or a lawful request from a competent authority.

9. Data Retention and Deletion

Wallora does not maintain user accounts or a developer-operated database containing users' personal information.

Preferences stored locally remain on the device until the user clears the App's data, deletes the relevant files or uninstalls the App.

Information processed by Google or other third-party providers is retained according to the policies and retention periods of those providers.

Support emails may be retained only for as long as reasonably necessary to respond to the request, maintain support records or meet legal obligations.

Users may request deletion of information previously sent directly to the developer by contacting: wallora.support@gmail.com .

10. Data Security

Reasonable organizational and technical measures are used to protect information handled in connection with the App.

However, no electronic transmission or storage method can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

11. Children's Privacy

Wallora is not specifically directed to children under the age required to independently consent to the processing of personal data in their country.

The developer does not knowingly request personal information from children.

A parent or legal guardian who believes that a child has provided personal information directly to the developer may contact wallora.support@gmail.com .

12. International Processing

Third-party services used by Wallora may process information in countries other than the user's country of residence. Such processing is governed by the safeguards and policies applied by those service providers.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated when the App, applicable laws or third-party services change.

The updated version will be published on this page together with a revised "Last updated" date.
